Dr. Abdus Salam

A pakistani physicist and nobel laureate

Here's a timeline of Dr. Abdus Salam's life

  • 1926 - Born in Jhang, Punjab, British India (present-day Punjab, Pakistan)
  • 1940 - At age 14, Salam scored the highest marks ever recorded for the matriculation (entrance) examination at the Punjab University.[22] He won a full scholarship to the Government College University of Lahore, Punjab State.
  • 1946 - Received his Master in Mathematics from the Government College University.
  • 1949 - he was awarded a scholarship to St John's College, Cambridge, where he completed a BA degree with Double First-Class Honours in Mathematics and Physics.
    • 1950 - He received the Smith's Prize from Cambridge University for the most outstanding pre-doctoral contribution to Physics.
  • 1958 - His doctoral thesis titled "Developments in quantum theory of fields" contained comprehensive and fundamental work in quantum electrodynamics and gained him an international reputation and the Adams Prize.
  • 1959 - Salam is recipient of first high civil award Star of Pakistan awarded (Sitara-e-Pakistan) awarded by the President of Pakistan for Salams outstanding services to Pakistan
  • 1960 - Salam was scientific advisor to the Ministry of Science and Technology in Pakistan from 1960 to 1974, a position from which he played a major and influential role in the development of the country's science infrastructure. Salam contributed to numerous developments in theoretical and particle physics in Pakistan.
  • 1964 - He was awarded Hughes Medal by Royal Society, London.
  • 1968 - He was aliso awarded Atoms for Peace Prize in 1968.
  • 1972 - As Science Advisor, Salam played a role in Pakistan's development of nuclear energy, and contributed as well, via Project-706, to the development of Pakistan's atomic bomb project in 1972, for he is viewed as the "scientific father" of this program.
  • 1978 - He was awarded Royal Medal by Royal Society, London.
  • 1978 - Same year he was awarded another medal for his contrtibutions, Matteucci Medal.
  • 1979 - He shared the 1979 Nobel Prize in Physics with Sheldon Glashow and Steven Weinberg for his contribution to the electroweak unification theory. He was the first Pakistani to receive a Nobel Prize in science.
